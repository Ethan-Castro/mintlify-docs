CSI HPCC operates on a cost-recovery model. Every user falls into one of four access plans that determine pricing, priority, and how compute time is purchased. Your PI or department typically selects the plan that fits the project budget and workload.

<Info>
  The figures below reflect rates published on the HPCC Wiki. Contact the [HPC Helpline](mailto:HPCHelp@csi.cuny.edu) or the HPCC Director to confirm current pricing before committing budget.
</Info>

## Plans at a glance

| Plan | Best for | Annual fee | Priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **MAP**: Minimal Access Plan | PIs with ongoing but modest compute needs | $5k / $15k / $25k (Tier A/B/C) | Standard |
| **CODP**: Compute on Demand Plan | Occasional or burst usage; no annual commitment | Pay per use | Standard |
| **LNP**: Lease Node Plan | Groups needing guaranteed capacity for a period | Negotiated | High |
| **Condo** | Labs purchasing permanent dedicated hardware | Capital + maintenance | Highest |

---

## MAP: Minimal Access Plan

MAP is a yearly subscription that pre-purchases a block of compute time at a discounted rate. Three tiers are available:

| Tier | Annual Fee | Included compute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Tier A** | $5,000 / yr | General allocation; no included free hours |
| **Tier B** | $15,000 / yr | 11,520 free CPU-hours + 1,440 free GPU-hours for new projects |
| **Tier C** | $25,000 / yr | 11,520 free CPU-hours + 1,440 free GPU-hours for new projects |

### MAP usage rates

| Resource | MAP rate |
| --- | --- |
| CPU-hour | $0.015 / hr |
| GPU-hour | $0.090 / hr |

MAP subscribers receive discounted rates versus CODP and are guaranteed capacity on shared partitions. Hours outside the included block are billed at the MAP rate.

---

## CODP: Compute on Demand Plan

CODP has no annual commitment. You pay only for what you use, at a slightly higher rate than MAP.

| Resource | CODP rate |
| --- | --- |
| CPU-hour | $0.018 / hr |
| GPU-hour | $0.110 / hr |

CODP is well suited for:

- New projects exploring the cluster before committing to MAP.
- Occasional users with low and unpredictable compute demand.
- Burst usage beyond a MAP allocation's included hours.

---

## LNP: Lease Node Plan

LNP provides exclusive access to one or more nodes for a negotiated period. Researchers pay a lease fee that covers reservation, power, cooling, and maintenance for the duration of the lease. Pricing depends on node type and duration; contact the Director for a quote.

---

## Condo ownership

Research groups with sustained, heavy compute needs can purchase hardware outright and have it housed, powered, cooled, and administered inside the HPCC facility. Condo owners receive:

- **Highest priority** scheduling on their own nodes.
- Access to the broader Arrow infrastructure (networking, storage, software) for jobs that spill beyond their condo allocation.

Current condo partitions on Arrow:

| Partition | Owner group | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `partchem` | Chemistry | No time limit; GPU types listed as A100/80 and A30. |
| `partcfd` | CFD group | No time limit; GPU type listed as A40. |
| `partsym` | Symmetry / applied math | No time limit; GPU type listed as A30. |
| `partasrc` | ASRC / atmospheric sciences | No time limit; GPU type listed as A30. |
| `partphys` | Physics | No time limit; GPU type listed as L40. |

Non-condo users can access condo nodes during idle periods through preemptible jobs. Ask the helpline whether this applies to your project.

## General-access partitions

All plans share the following general-access partitions:

| Partition | Wall-clock limit | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `partnsf` | 240 hours | Advanced-tier partition with K20m, V100/16, and A100/40 GPUs listed by HPCC. |
| `partmatlabD` | 240 hours | MATLAB daytime partition with V100/16 and A100/40 GPUs listed by HPCC. |
| `partmatlabN` | 240 hours | MATLAB nighttime partition for CPU-only MATLAB work. |
| `partdev` | 4 hours | Development and short test jobs on a 16-core node with 64 GB memory and 2 K20m GPUs. |

## Storage pricing

Storage beyond the default free tier is billed separately:

| Storage tier | Rate |
| --- | --- |
| Online scratch (first 50 GB) | **Free** for all users |
| Additional scratch storage | $60 / TB / year |
| Backup storage | $45 / TB / year |
| Archive (tape-class) | $35 / TB / year |

The default home directory quota is 50 GB / 10,000 files. To purchase additional storage, contact the [HPC Helpline](mailto:HPCHelp@csi.cuny.edu) with the amount you need and the project it's associated with.

## Choosing a plan

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I'm a new PI starting a funded project" icon="flask">
    Start with **CODP** to understand your usage, then move to **MAP Tier B or C** once you have a budget and know your compute demand. New MAP-B and MAP-C projects receive a block of free hours to get started.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I'm a student or postdoc with a shared PI allocation" icon="graduation-cap">
    Your PI's MAP or CODP account covers your usage. You submit jobs under your own username, and hours are billed to the PI's project. You don't need your own plan, just an approved account.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My lab runs 24/7 high-priority jobs" icon="server">
    Consider **LNP** (for a fixed period of exclusivity) or **Condo** (to permanently own your nodes). Both give higher scheduling priority and predictable capacity.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I only need the cluster occasionally for teaching" icon="chalkboard-user">
    **CODP** is the right default. Class accounts are provisioned at no cost up to the approved quota; contact the helpline to set them up before the semester.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
